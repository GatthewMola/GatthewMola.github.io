The purpose of this database is to compile Hood College Men’s Lacrosse alumni data so that all past and future alumni have access to a network of professionals in multiple industries they are already familiar with. This database contains the hitting, pitching, and fielding stats for a number of players on the 2023 Baltimore Orioles roster. I created this database as an exercise to better hone my skills in table creation, value insertion, join methods, and indexing/pagination. Like the Alumni Network database, the Orioles database was created using PostgreSQL in pgAdmin. Completed code is transferred to VSCode for upload to Git repository.

The Effect of the Stringency of Environmental Regulations on Greenhouse Gas Emissions in Developing Countries - 4/12/2021

The purpose of my Economics Capstone paper was not only to display my knowledge of economic concepts and theories, but also to show my ability to seek out interesting research opportunities and determine the best methods to analyze them. Throughout the latter half of my college career I became very passionate about climate initiatives in government policy and their effects on the environment. This research paper is the culmination of that passion. I employed the use of omitted variable and multivariate pooled regressions with two-way fixed effects to understand the effect of environmental regulations in developing countries. Developing countries tend to support more pollution-producing industries than developed countries due to a lack of available carbon-capture technologies and/or lack the economic incentives to support cleaner forms of industry. Therefore, environmental regulations in these countries tend to have a greater impact on their respective environments. This paper aimed to determine this effect on ten different developing economies in multiple climates.

The Effect of Collective Bargaining Legislation on Public Sector Wages - A Literature Review and Cross-Sectional Study - 5/1/2020

Another great passion of mine in government policy is collective bargaining and the effects of unions in the private and government sectors. I come from a family of teachers who taught me that collective bargaining in the form of unions were their greatest asset when it came to promoting higher wages, increased benefits, and more comfortable work environments. It was not until I took Labor Economics and Indsutrial Organization my Junior year of college that I had the knowledge or the capability to understand how and why unions are able to support their workforces so effectively. This paper represents the concepts and methods I learned regarding collective bargaining and it’s ability to leverage worker sentiment to produce better results from contract negotiation (specifically in the public sector). For this paper, I also employed omitted variable and multivariate regressions, albeit significantly less complex versions than in the previous paper as this study was primarily one into the literature of the topic.

A Cross-Sectional Study on the Effect of State Minimum Wage on Youth Unemployment at the State Level - 12/14/2019

Much in the same vain of the collective bargaining paper that follows it, my very first econometrics paper analyzed the effect of wage minima instead of union influence on unemployment.

I was heavily influenced by David Card and Alan Krueger’s landmark 1995 study on the effect of minimum wage changes between two states within the same metropolitan area (New Jersey and Pennsylvania-Philadelphia). Card and Krueger found that when New Jersey raised their state minimum wage higher than Pennsylvania, employment in New Jersey rose compared to that of Pennsylvania and the Philadelphia area. It was theorized that workers chose in the Philadelphia area chose to employ themselves at the same level in the neighboring New Jersey area as the wages had now increased. This method exploiting a natural experiment intrigued me so much that I hypothesized the same effect across multiple states when examining youth unemployment alone. I theorized that youth unemployment would be more affected by changes in minimum wage as their inexperience and limited skillset generally forces them to rely on positions that only pay minimum wage.

While my findings were unsurprisingly statistically insignificant, I learned a valuable lesson in my early college career that econometric theories or findings in niche situations cannot always be extrapolated upon an outsized and far more variant dataset.
